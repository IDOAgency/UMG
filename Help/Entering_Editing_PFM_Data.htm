Entering/Editing PFM Data

Creating and editing PFMs follow the same basic procedure as using the Selection and BOM screens. Use the Notepad to select a selection requiring a PFM. Enter data into the form as needed. You can save your work in draft or final status and you can copy saved forms. You also can print saved forms and/or attach them to e-mails. When PFMs are saved in Final status, e-mail notifications are sent automatically to the appropriate personnel.

Follow these steps to complete PFMs:

1.      Click on the PFM menu option, located directly beneath the Labels tab, to access the PFM screen.

2.      Using the Notepad, scroll to or search for the product for which you wish to enter or edit the PFM form.

3.      Click once anywhere on the product title in the Notepad to select (highlight) it. The PFM form appears, pink for digital products, blue for physical.

Keep in mind…

·        For physical products, entries in fields from the Selection screen are read-only. Changes made to them must be made in the Selection screen – with three exceptions: Spine Title, Spine Artist and Music Type. Entries in these three fields, originally taken fro the Selection screen, can be edited directly from the physical PFM..

·        For digital products, some fields taken from the Selection screen – Config Code, Music Type and UPC – can be edited in either Selection or digital PFM screen.

·        Default entries in Submitted By, Phone, Fax, and Email fields are taken from the User Info screen but can be overwritten on the PFM form as needed.

4.      Go to the Draft and Final radio buttons at top. Draft is selected by default. Click Final if your PFM is complete and ready. Keep in mind that a form in final status, once saved, can trigger e-mail notification.

5.      To enter comments, click the paper icon next to the Comments field. A popup window provides a text box for your comments.

6.      Enter text in the text box as needed.

7.      Do one of the following:

·        Click Save Comments.

·        Or click Cancel to return without saving.

8.      Complete your PFM, adding entries in remaining fields as needed. Where available, use pulldown lists to choose entries.

9.      Click Save (Draft) or Save+Send (Final) to save your PFM. The screen refreshes. A copy icon should now appear at top left. 

Copying PFM forms

Any saved PFM can be copied, then pasted into any other PFM which has not yet been saved. Follow these steps:

1.      Go to the PFM you wish to use as your original. See Creating PFMs, above, if you need to create one.

2.      Click the Copy icon (shown). The screen refreshes and PFM data is copied.

3.      Using the Notepad, scroll to or search for the PFM to which you wish to copy your original. The Paste icon appears, upper left.

4.      Click the Paste icon (shown left). The screen refreshes. Entries from your original PFM are pasted to the current screen.

5.      Make changes to fields as needed. Then click Save or Save+Send and continue.
