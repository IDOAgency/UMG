Creating Schedules

When you first save a product in the Selection screen – either physical or digital – you are taken immediately to the Schedule screen for the product you’ve saved. On the Schedule screen are listed schedule templates, recommended based on the product category, release type, format/schedule type and sub-format. To create a schedule for your product, do one of the following:

·        Select a template

·        Search for a template using the search function

·        Search for and copy an existing release’s schedule.

Procedures for all three methods are given below.

Selecting a Recommended Template

To select a Milestone recommended template, follow these steps:

1.      Click the radio button to the left of the template name. The screen refreshes and the tasks pre-defined for the selected template display in the summary section. Due Dates and Weeks are calculated based on pre-defined specifications and the product’s Street/Ship Date.

2.      Click the Save button, (there are two: one just under the menu bar at top, the other at the bottom of the screen) to save and apply the template to your product.

Important

Always remember to click Save when you’ve selected a template. If you leave the Schedule screen without saving, no confirmation message is displayed and your template is not applied.

Searching for templates

Follow these steps to use the search function to locate and retrieve schedule templates for your products. Do this if the templates Milestone suggests do not meet your requirements.

1.      Click the Search link (shown left) next to the Select a Template option. The Schedule Template Search dialog (also shown) appears.

2.      Enter search criteria using available fields, including:

·        Template Name, a text box

·        Format/Schedule Type pulldown

·        Product Category pulldown

3.      Click Submit Search. Templates matching your criteria are displayed (shown).

4.      Click the radio button next to the template you wish to use. The screen refreshes and the tasks pre-defined for the selected template display in the summary section. Due Dates and Weeks are calculated based on pre-defined specifications and the Street/Ship Date.

5.      Click the Save button, (there are two: one just under the menu bar at top, the other at the bottom of the screen) to save and apply the template.

 

Searching for and copying existing schedules

Follow these steps to copy existing schedules and apply them to your products.

1.      Click the Search link (shown left) next to the Copy a Release Schedule option. The Selection Search dialog (also shown) appears.

2.      Enter search criteria using available fields.

·        Click the Include Closed/Cancel checkbox if you wish to include closed or cancelled products with your search results.

3.      Click Submit Search. Templates matching your criteria are displayed (shown).

4.      Click the radio button next to the template you wish to use. The screen refreshes and the tasks pre-defined for the template are displayed. Due Dates and Weeks are calculated based on pre-defined specifications and product release date.

5.      Click the Save button, (there are two: one just under the menu bar at top, the other at the bottom of the screen) to save and apply the template.

 

 

Working with displayed schedules

Once schedules have been created and saved, you are given several options for configuring the display to your requirements.

·        You can filter displayed tasks by department

·        You can filter displayed tasks by owner

·        You can sort tasks using any of the displayed columns as sort key

Procedures for all these are given below.

Applying task filters

The display of the Schedule screen can be filtered by department and/or task owner. In the example above, the owner filter has been applied to show only label tasks. Follow these steps:

·        To filter by department, pull down the Department list and select the department by which you wish to filter your display.

·        To filter by owner, pull down the Owner list and select the task owner by which you wish to filter your display. Your choices include All, Only Label Tasks and Only UML Tasks for physical products, Only eCommerce Tasks for digital products..

The screen refreshes and displays only those tasks meeting your filter criteria.

Note

A default filter can be set-up for each user.  The filter can also be disabled for users if a user does not require the ability to change it. Contact your system administrator for details.

Sorting task displays

Any column heading within the schedule can be used to sort displayed tasks. In the example above, notice tasks are sorted by the Task Assignment column. Simply click the blue column heading hyperlink for the column by which you wish to sort. The screen refreshes and tasks are sorted as you specify. Default sort sequence is by WKS (Weeks to release) in descending  order. 

Adding tasks to schedules

In the section above, procedures are given for bringing entire sets of tasks into the Schedule screen. Once this has been done, you can add individual tasks, as needed. Follow the steps listed below.

Assigning Individual Tasks

1.      Click the Switch to Tasks button at the bottom of the Notepad screen. The screen refreshes and the Notepad now lists pre-defined tasks.

2.      Use the navigation buttons to scroll to the task you wish to use. Or click the Search button and search for specific tasks.

3.      Click the checkbox next to the task you wish to apply. You can click multiple tasks on the same page, but once you move to another page, your checkbox entries are lost.

4.      Click the Assign Tasks button to bring the tasks you select into the current schedule. When you do, those tasks are removed from the Notepad to prevent adding duplicate tasks.

5.      Click Save in the Selection screen to save your changes.

Deleting assigned tasks

You can also remove tasks from schedules as needed. Keep in mind tasks with entered completion dates and/or status entries of Done or N/A cannot be deleted unless you first reverse those entries.

Deleting all tasks

You can delete all schedule tasks in a single operation.

1.      Do one of the following, depending on whether the schedule has been saved first.

·        If the Schedule screen has not been saved, simply re-select the product. To do this, click once on the product entry in the Notepad. The screen refreshes and schedule tasks are cleared.

·        If the Schedule screen has been saved, click the Delete button (upper right). All tasks are deleted – provided no entries have been made in any columns.  A warning dialog is displayed if you attempt to delete tasks for which entries have been made.

Deleting individual tasks

Follow these steps to delete schedule tasks individually. Keep in mind the delete link appears only on task lines for which no entries have been made. Also, label personnel can delete only label tasks; tasks assigned to UML or eCommerce must be deleted by UML or eCommerce personnel, respectively.

1.      Click the blue del link (shown above) between Status and Vendor columns for the task you wish to delete. A confirmation dialog appears.

2.      Click OK to delete the task. Click Cancel to return without deleting.

3.      Click Save from the Schedule screen to save your changes.

Editing due dates assigned to tasks

You can the edit system-calculated due dates assigned to tasks as needed. Use one of these methods:

·        Delete and enter new (and specific) due dates. When you do this, the weeks to release entry for these tasks are cleared, to reduce confusion and the possibility of recalculating a changed date.

·        Change the weeks or days to release entries, then use the Recalc button to recalculate all due dates.

Procedures for both methods are given below.

Entering new due dates

Follow these steps to change due dates assigned to tasks:

1.      Move your cursor to the due date you wish to change.

2.      Delete the existing entry.

3.      Enter a new due date using any of the following formats:

·        mm/dd/yy

·        mm-dd-yy

·        mm.dd.yy

You can also use the handy pop-up calendar to enter dates. To do this, double click on the date field and the calendar pop-up appears.

Use the scroll buttons to move to the month and/or year of the date you wish to use.

Click once on the date you wish to use. The schedule screen returns, the date you specify appears in the Due date field.

Keep in mind….

If the due date is past today’s date, the background of the due date field turns pink.

Changing weeks/days to release entries

Follow these steps to change weeks or days to release and recalculate due dates:

1.      To clear all entries in the Wks column (optional) click the Clear button. A confirmation dialog appears. Do one of the following:

·        Click OK to clear all entries. The Schedule screen returns. All editable fields in the Wks and Due columns are blank.

·        Click Cancel to return to previous display without making any changes.

2.      Enter new weeks or days to release in the Wks column for all tasks you wish to change. Use the format NN 99, where n represents the day of the week (M, T, W, TH, F) and 99 represents the number of weeks before release.

3.      Click Save. The screen refreshes.

4.      Click Recalc. A confirmation dialog (shown) appears. Do one of the following:

·        Click Incomplete Tasks Only to recalculate only due dates assigned to tasks for which no completed dates have been entered.

·        Click All Tasks to recalculate due dates for all tasks, regardless of whether completed dates have been entered. Your completed dates are not cleared when you use this option.

·        Click Cancel to return to the previous screen without recalculating any dates.

5.      Click Save to save your changes.
