Editing Selections

In general, editing Milestone product records on the Selection screen follows the same basic framework as most Milestone processes. Locate the product in the Notepad, go to the Selection screen, make your changes, then save. Follow these steps:

1.      Click Selection in the menu bar under the Labels tab. (Typically, Labels tab is activated by default when you log on.) The Selection screen appears.

2.      In the Notepad, search for or navigate to a product for which you have edit rights. See Basic Training for procedures on locating products in the Notepad.

3.      Click anywhere on the product title in the Notepad to select (highlight) it. Data for the selected product appears in the Selection screen.

Making changes to project information

4.      Make changes to fields according to your requirements.

5.      To edit project fields (Environment, Imprint, Company, Division, Label, Operating Company, Super Label, Sub Label or Project ID), click the REVISE button. The Project Search window opens. Enter search criteria, then click Search or press Enter. Projects matching your criteria are listed. Double click on the project you wish to select.

6.      Once all changes have been made, click Save to save your work.

Editing Local Product Numbers

Local Product Numbers, once they’ve been generated, also can be changed as needed. Depending on whether you’ve assigned schedule to the products, though, the procedures for doing so might vary slightly. Follow these steps:

·        When no schedule or completion dates have been assigned to your product, simply delete the selection and start the entire process again. That is, re-create the product and use the auto-generate function to get a new Local Product Number. Be sure to inform your UML contact about this deletion, so that Legacy can be updated.

·        When tasks and completion dates have been assigned already, your changes must be made manually. Keep in mind the following:

o       If you’re changing Config Code and the solicitation sheet has not yet gone out, make your changes to the Config Code field and to the configuration digit of the Local Product Number. Be sure to click Save when changes are complete. And be sure to notify your UML contact per your normal procedures, particularly if your PFM is not in Final status.

o       If you’re changing Config Code and the solicitation sheet has already gone out, make your changes to the Config Code field only. At this point, changing the local product number might cause confusion among UMVD and retailers. Be sure to click Save when changes are complete. And be sure to notify your UML contact per your normal procedures, particularly if your PFM is not in Final status.

Keep in mind…

UML needs to be sent an updated PFM whenever changes are made to physical products. If the PFM has been marked as Final (and the label has been set up for automatic e-mail notifications), Milestone generates and sends the updated PFM automatically. If not, you must generate an updated PFM and send it to your UML contact manually.
